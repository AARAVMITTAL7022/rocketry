# 🚀 Rocket Model Simulation (MATLAB)

> A MATLAB-based simulation of a single-stage rocket analyzing vertical flight dynamics, forces, and parameter sensitivity.

---

## 📄 Overview

This project focuses on modeling and simulating the motion of a **single-stage rocket** under simplified conditions. The simulation studies how different physical parameters affect the rocket’s trajectory, especially its **maximum altitude (apogee)**.

The model assumes **1D vertical motion** and applies fundamental physics principles to understand rocket behavior.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Develop a **MATLAB simulation** for rocket motion
* Validate simulation results using analytical methods
* Analyze how different parameters influence rocket altitude

The modeled rocket consists of:

* Nose cone

## ⚙️ Physics & Forces

Three main forces act on the rocket:

* **Thrust (T):** Upward force generated by the engine
* **Gravity (mg):** Downward force due to weight
* **Drag (D):** Air resistance opposing motion

The governing equation:

$$
m(t)\frac{dv}{dt} = T - mg - D
$$

Drag force:

$$
D = \frac{1}{2} \rho C_d A v^2
$$

Where:

* (\rho) = air density
* (C_d) = drag coefficient
* (A) = cross-sectional area
* (v) = velocity

---

## 🛫 Flight Phases

### 1. Burn Phase

* Fuel is consumed
* Mass decreases over time
* Thrust accelerates the rocket

### 2. Coasting (Ballistic Phase)

* Thrust becomes zero
* Rocket continues upward due to inertia

### 3. Apogee

* Maximum height reached
* Velocity becomes zero

### 4. Descent

* Gravity pulls rocket downward

---

## 🧮 Simulation Method

The simulation follows these steps:

1. Define rocket parameters and constants
2. Initialize altitude, velocity, and mass
3. Compute forces at each time step
4. Calculate acceleration using Newton’s law
5. Update velocity and altitude iteratively
6. Continue until the rocket returns to ground

### 📊 Outputs

* Altitude vs Time
* Velocity vs Time
* Mass vs Time

---

## ✅ Numerical Validation

The simulation is validated by comparing apogee with an analytical model (ignoring drag):

$$
h_{max} = h_b + \frac{v_b^2}{2g}
$$

* Analytical result gives **higher altitude**
* Confirms drag is correctly implemented in simulation

## 📈 Sensitivity Analysis

The following parameters were varied:

* Thrust
* Drag Coefficient
* Initial Mass
* Propellant Mass
* Rocket Diameter

### 🔍 Key Findings

| Parameter        | Effect on Altitude   |
| ---------------- | -------------------- |
| Thrust           | ↑ increases altitude |
| Drag Coefficient | ↑ decreases altitude |
| Initial Mass     | ↑ decreases altitude |
| Propellant Mass  | ↑ increases altitude |
| Rocket Diameter  | ↑ decreases altitude |

### 🏆 Sensitivity Ranking

1. Thrust
2. Drag Coefficient
3. Initial Mass
4. Propellant Mass
5. Rocket Diameter

---

## ⚠️ Limitations

This is a simplified model with assumptions:

* Only vertical motion considered
* No wind effects
* Constant drag coefficient
* Constant air density
* Constant thrust
* No rotation dynamics
* Launch effects ignored

---

## 🧠 Conclusion

The project successfully simulates rocket motion and demonstrates how **thrust and drag** are the most critical factors affecting rocket altitude.

It provides a strong foundation for understanding **basic rocket physics** and numerical simulation techniques.
